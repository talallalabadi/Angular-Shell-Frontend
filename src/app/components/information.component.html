DigiClips® Information

A comprehensive media results platform purpose-built to capture every piece of media data that legacy tools miss.

DigiClips® is an improved media results software system designed to solve one problem that traditional search engines and media monitoring tools still fail to address: they miss data. The current systems used in the media industry are outdated, incomplete, and unable to capture the full scope of television, radio, print, streaming, and online content. DigiClips was created to eliminate missed content by integrating speech-to-text, text recognition, video and image recognition, and a modern relational database designed specifically to process media data more accurately than the older platforms still in use today.

The software processes content from television, radio, newspapers, magazines, blogs, social media, podcasts, web TV, and SiriusXM, producing Media Results Reports that include segment text, timing, sentiment, audience numbers, publicity value, media value, markets, ratings, captions, translations, and more. Users can download, edit, and transfer segments from the original sources, giving them real control over the media they analyze. DigiClips also includes a full administration system that tracks machines, hardware activity, missed data, and system errors — features that industry-standard systems simply do not provide.

The platform supports multiple types of users. The General Public version is supported by advertising revenue and gives free users limited access to the system, including basic searches and previews. Subscribers gain the full suite of tools such as detailed Media Results Reports, email alerts for keywords and subjects, segment editing, export tools, search history, and deeper analytical insights. Administrators are given the ability to approve new users, manage subscribers, monitor system performance, and oversee all aspects of the media ingestion pipeline.

DigiClips exists because media monitoring today is fragmented and shallow; existing systems struggle to identify context, narrative patterns, or reputational risk, and require enormous manual labor to interpret results. DigiClips aims to replace that manual process with a modern, user-friendly platform that increases the percentage of positive search results, captures context across all media sources, and gives users meaningful insight into why a story appears and how it spreads. By combining multiple university teams, ongoing research, and continuous improvements, DigiClips is positioned to become a more complete and more accurate media awareness system than any of the existing industry tools.
